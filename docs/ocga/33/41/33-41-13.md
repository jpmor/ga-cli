# § 33-41-13. Reserves

(a) Every captive insurance company shall maintain reserves in an amount estimated in the aggregate to provide for the payment of all unpaid losses and claims incurred, whether reported or unreported, for which such captive insurance company may be liable, together with the expenses of adjustment or settlement of such losses and claims. Every captive insurance company shall keep a complete and itemized record, in a form satisfactory to the Commissioner, showing all losses and claims on which it has received notice.
(b) If the loss experience of a captive insurance company shows that its loss reserves, however estimated, are inadequate, the Commissioner shall require the captive insurance company to maintain increased amounts of loss reserves as are needed to make its loss reserves adequate.
(c) Every captive insurance company shall maintain an unearned premium reserve on all policies in force which shall never be less in the aggregate than the captive insurance company's actual liability to all its insureds for the return of gross unearned premiums computed pursuant to the method commonly referred to as the monthly pro rata method.
